Empowering The Future: A Literacy Rate Analysis For A Better Tomorrow Literacy is a vital indicator of economic development, reflecting the progress of a society. In India, a person aged seven and above who can read and write in any language is considered literate for census purposes. Literacy plays a crucial role in a nation's economic growth. Despite India's significant improvement in literacy rates, currently standing at 74.04% (2021) compared to a mere 12% during Independence in 1947, it still falls short of the global average of 84%. India is home to the largest illiterate population among all nations.
